    Russian law

    On 1 September 2015, a new law comes into force in the Russian Federation - “On Amendments to Certain Laws of the Russian Federation in Order to Clarify the Procedure for Personal Data Processing in Information and Telecommunications Networks”.

    Under this new law companies collecting data of Russian citizens are obliged to perform any action related to the personal data of the Russian clients in databases located within the territory of the Russian Federation.

    Even if you conduct your business online (i.e. web shops, hotel/airline reservations, social networking, etc.) you are still obligated to store the personal data of the Russian clients on a server situated in Russia.

    Personal data refers to any information that identifies a specific person.

    In order to comply with the law there are the following options:

    — Collocate your server on the territory on the Russian Federation

    — Rent a physical or virtual server on the territory of the Russian Federation

    — Install a server in your own premises in Russia if applicable.

    Failing to comply with the law may lead to the administrative liability for violation as well as the company information being entered into a blacklist. On top of that, the website might be blocked from operating in Russia.
